\section*{Strategic Planning Tool}
\begin{table}[h!]
    \centering
    \small
    \begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{|X|X|}
        \hline
        \textbf{Box 1: Problem \& Stakeholders} \newline 
        Stakeholders: Researchers and educators. Decision informed: Generalize sequential decision-making under uncertainty, permanent consequences, and constraints. Main outcome: A reusable experimental benchmark environment for evaluating RL algorithms. & 
        \textbf{Box 2: Context \& Project Type} \newline 
        Research and Tech Development. Developing a reusable experimental framework to study generalization, robustness, and risk-aware decision making in RL. \\ \hline
        
        \textbf{Box 3: Describe the Data} \newline 
        Source: Pokémon Showdown engine accessed via \texttt{poke-env}. Formats: Battle state vectors, configuration files (gauntlet specifications), and wikis. Translated into fixed-size numeric embedding vectors. Quality: High precision simulation data from Showdown engine. & 
        \textbf{Box 4: ML Approach} \newline 
        Reinforcement Learning (e.g., Curriculum Learning) using Transformers or MLPs. Justification: Sequential decision-making problem with substantial stochasticity where deterministic solutions cannot be simply memorized, due to state space.\\ \hline
        
        \textbf{Box 5: ML Pipeline (DSPRO2)} \newline 
        Collection (Showdown engine logs via \texttt{poke-env}) $\rightarrow$ Preparation (Vector embeddings creation) $\rightarrow$ Model Training (\texttt{Ray RLlib}) $\rightarrow$ Evaluation (Consistent evaluation protocols/metrics) $\rightarrow$ Iterate. & 
        \textbf{Box 6: Success Definition} \newline 
        Technical metric: Mean episode reward, policy/value losses, entropy. \newline Stakeholder KPI: Gauntlet completion rate, survival rate, wipe probability, average number of Pokémon lost. \\ \hline
        
        \textbf{Box 7: Risks \& Guardrails} \newline 
        Minimal risk regarding EU AI Act rules. No privacy data or biased decisions affecting humans (game simulation only). Guardrails involve robust curriculum stages to ensure gradual, verifiable learning rather than overfitting. & 
        \textbf{Box 8: Infrastructure (DSPRO2)} \newline 
        Primary development locally, scaling training locally or HSLU GPUhub computing resources. Employs memory optimization techniques (Ray Plasma). MLFlow for experiment tracking. \\ \hline
        
        \textbf{Box 9: Costs \& Timeline (DSPRO2)} \newline 
        Costs: Electricity and working hours. \newline Timeline: DSPRO2 FS26 (approx. 10--12 weeks). & 
        \textbf{Box 10: Team \& Next Steps (DSPRO2)} \newline 
        Team setup via GitHub Projects (Backlog, To Do, In Progress, Review, Done). Next Step: Start team building agent architecture, \texttt{poke-env} bridging, setting up curriculum and basic Transformer policy training loops. \\ \hline
    \end{tabularx}
\end{table}
